Caramel & Chocolate Tarts

Recipe by Ravneet Gill

This deliciously morish tart balances sweet chocolate ganache with deeply salty miso caramel and with its no-bake base and fridge-set filling, it's a breeze to put together. Use any cereal flakes you like, but for an added nuttiness Ravneet suggests using bran.

Ingredients

  • 190g 70% dark chocolate
  • 47g unsalted butter
  • 30g roasted hazelnuts, chopped
  • 90g cereal flakes, crushed
  • pinch of sea salt
  • 35g caster sugar
  • 465ml double cream
  • 1 tbsp white miso paste
  • 60g milk chocolate
  • 15g icing sugar
  • Â½ tsp vanilla bean extract

Steps

  1. For the tart cases, melt the chocolate and 40g butter in a bowl set over a pan of simmering water. In a separate bowl, mix the remaining ingredients together then pour in the melted chocolate mixture. Stir until combined.
  2. Divide the mixture between the six tart cases. Press into the bottom and up the sides of the cases, making sure you have a good edge to support your filling. Put into the fridge to set for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Heat the caster sugar in a saucepan over a medium heat to dissolve the sugar, swirling the pan from time to time, but do not stir. Once the sugar turns a deep amber, add the remaining butter and whisk well. Pour in 65ml cream and let bubble for a minute before removing from the heat and whisking in the miso.
  4. Let the caramel cool slightly in the pan, then pour into a heatproof dish. Leave to cool until it is pouring consistency, but not hot.
  5. Heat 200ml cream until it just begins to steam, but do not let it boil. Put the milk and remaining dark chocolate into a large bowl and pour over the hot cream, set aside for 1 minute. Stir with a whisk from the middle outwards, until melted and smooth. Pour into the cooled tart shells and set aside for 2 minutes.
    6. Drizzle the caramel over the ganache, then use a cocktail stick to create a marbled effect. Put in the fridge to set.
  6. Whisk together the remaining double cream, icing sugar and vanilla bean extract until soft peaks.
  7. Remove the tarts from their cases and serve with a quenelle of Chantilly cream.
